## Ingredients

### Batter
- Chicken thighs (boneless, skinless) — 500g
- Gochujang — 11g
- Soy sauce — 5ml
- Sesame oil — 8ml
- Erythritol or monk fruit sweetener — 6g
- White pepper — 1g
- Rice vinegar — 15ml
- Cornstarch — 56g (36g was too little — needed extra 20g for proper ribbon consistency)
- Cold water — 30–40ml to loosen

### Garlic Soy Finishing Sauce
- Neutral oil — 15ml
- Garlic — 4 cloves, grated
- Soy sauce — 15ml (reduced from 22ml — original was too salty)
- Sesame oil — 8ml
- Water — 20ml (increased from 15ml to compensate for reduced soy)

### Veg Bed (doubles as rack)
- Mushrooms — 128g, torn into chunks
- Courgette — 188g, sliced into 1cm half-moons
- Toss in oil only — skip soy on veg bed (adds unnecessary salt)

## Method Notes

**Batter:** Whisk gochujang, soy, sesame oil, sweetener, white pepper, rice vinegar and cornstarch until smooth. Add cold water gradually until ribbon consistency — coats back of spoon and drips slowly. Pat chicken completely dry, score 2–3 times, coat in batter, let excess drip off, straight onto veg bed in preheated oven.

**Veg bed:** Toss mushrooms and courgette in oil only. Spread on foil-lined tray. Chicken goes directly on top — veg acts as the rack, keeps chicken off the tray, absorbs drippings.

**Oven:** 220°C fan. Top third of oven. 15 mins, flip carefully, 10–12 mins more. Grill/broil final 3–4 mins to blister and crisp. Internal temp 75°C. Rest 5 mins before slicing.

**Finishing sauce:** While chicken rests, low heat pan, oil in, grated garlic, sweat 60–90 secs no colour. Add soy + water, stir 30 secs. Off heat, add sesame oil last. Toss hot chicken in sauce before slicing.

**Garnish:** Spring onion (thinly sliced) + white sesame on top before serving.

## Key Tips
- Pat chicken completely dry before battering — non-negotiable
- Batter to oven in under 2 mins — don't let it sit or it goes gummy
- Never cook sesame oil — always add off heat
- Grill last 3–4 mins is what gives you the crust — don't skip
- Mushrooms shrink massively — keep pieces large
- 56g cornstarch for 500g chicken is the correct ratio — 36g was insufficient
