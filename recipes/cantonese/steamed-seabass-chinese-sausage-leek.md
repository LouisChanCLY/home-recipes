## Ingredients
- Sea bass fillets — 360g (4 fillets)
- 臘腸 (Chinese sausage) — 39g, sliced thin
- Leek (white part) — ~100g, sliced into strips
- Steamed fish soy sauce — 10g
- Neutral oil — 1 tsp (for hot oil finish)
- Shaoxing wine — small splash

## Method Notes
Leek strips laid on the plate as a base to elevate the fillets and allow steam to circulate. Sea bass placed skin side up. Shaoxing wine and white pepper applied lightly before steaming. Chinese sausage slices placed on top of the fish so fat renders down into the flesh during steaming.

Steam on high heat (water boiling first) for 5-6 minutes depending on fillet thickness. Check with chopstick at the thickest point. Discard excess liquid from the plate after steaming. Pile fresh leek strips on top, then pour hot oil over to sizzle. Finish with steamed fish soy sauce — use less than usual as the Chinese sausage is already salty.

Note: 4 fillets won't fit in one steamer layer — steam in 2 batches or use 2 plates.

## Ideas & Variations
- Ginger slices work better than leek as the base for stronger flavour and aroma — use when available
- Could try lap cheong + dried shrimp combo for extra umami
- Chilli oil instead of plain oil for the finish adds a kick

## Pairings
- Jasmine rice (70g raw per portion)
- Stir-fried carrot with leek greens as a side
