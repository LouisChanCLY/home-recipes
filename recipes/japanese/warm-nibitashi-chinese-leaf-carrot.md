## Ingredients (2 portions)
- Chinese leaf — 403g, cut into 5cm pieces
- Carrots — 106g, thin julienne or peeler ribbons
- Dashi stock — 300ml (instant dashi powder fine)
- Mirin — 2 tbsp
- Light soy sauce — 1.5 tbsp

## Method Notes
Make dashi broth first — simmer dashi, mirin, soy together. Keep on lowest heat.
Blanch carrot julienne 60 sec in lightly salted boiling water, remove into warm broth.
Blanch Chinese leaf 30 sec, remove, cool slightly, squeeze out excess water with hands.
Add squeezed Chinese leaf into broth with carrot — both veg and broth should be warm.
At 8 min mark: lift Chinese leaf, squeeze again, return to broth. This re-soak concentrates flavour.
Keep on lowest heat throughout. Serve warm — lift veg with tongs, let excess broth drain before plating.

Served warm tonight (cold night). Room temp version: cool broth and veg together 45-60 min, squeeze-resoak at 15 min mark.

## Ideas & Variations
- Add a few drops of sesame oil to the broth for warmth
- Tofu cubes could go in the broth alongside the veg
- Blanched spinach works as a third vegetable

## Pairings
- Saba no Miso Ni — the light dashi broth contrasts the heavy miso glaze well
- Any Japanese fish or chicken dish
