## Ingredients (2 portions)
- Mackerel fillets x2 (~180g total)
- White wine — 72.8g (substitute for sake)
- Mirin — 7.7g
- Water — 70ml
- Sweetener — 1 tsp (substitute for sugar)
- Red miso — 19g
- White miso — 10g
- Ginger powder — 1/4 tsp
- Ginger yuzu vinegar — 1 tsp (added off heat at end)

## Method Notes
Shimofuri the fillets (30 sec boiling water, cold rinse, pat dry) before braising.
Pre-mix miso with warm water until smooth — add to pan halfway through, never at the start.
Otoshibuta (foil lid with holes) for first 6 min. Remove foil, add miso, continue 4 min basting constantly.
Plate fish, reduce sauce to glossy lacquer, add yuzu vinegar off heat, spoon over fish.
Rest 3 min before serving.

White wine works well in place of sake — slightly less umami depth but good result.
29g miso (down from 43g) gives a cleaner, less overpowering sauce.

## Ideas & Variations
- Try proper sake next time for fuller depth
- Fresh ginger (sliced in braise + grated at end) would elevate further
- Blended miso 60/40 red to white is the sweet spot

## Pairings
- Warm nibitashi (Chinese leaf and carrot in dashi broth)
- Short grain rice
